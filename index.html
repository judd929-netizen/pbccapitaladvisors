How We Work

The Deal Process

A disciplined, four-phase approach to every transaction. Select your deal type below to see how we execute.

Phase 01

Preparation & Valuation

Engage PBC Capital, sign advisory agreement, gather and normalize financials, calculate valuation range, and review tax structure for the exit.

Phase 02

Marketing Materials

Draft Confidential Information Memorandum, management presentation, and financial model. Identify and prioritize strategic and financial buyer universe.

Phase 03

Competitive Process

Blind teaser outreach, NDA execution, CIM distribution, buyer Q&A, management meetings, and first-round IOI collection and analysis.

Phase 04

LOI, Diligence & Close

Negotiate LOI, manage due diligence data room, coordinate legal and accounting workstreams, resolve purchase price adjustments, and close.

Phase 01

Acquisition Strategy

Define deal criteria — size, sector, geography, EBITDA range, deal structure preferences, and platform vs. add-on considerations.

Phase 02

Target Identification

Proprietary sourcing via PBC Capital's network, direct outreach to off-market targets, and management introductions under confidentiality.

Phase 03

Financial Analysis

CPA-led Quality of Earnings, normalized EBITDA, tax exposure review, working capital assessment, and letter of intent structuring.

Phase 04

Negotiation & Close

Purchase price negotiation, asset vs. stock election, reps and warranties, escrow terms, closing mechanics, and integration planning.

Phase 01

Capital Assessment

Evaluate financing need, appropriate capital type (growth equity, senior debt, mezzanine, or hybrid), and impact on ownership structure.

Phase 02

Materials & Model

Build investor-grade offering memorandum, financial projections, and use-of-proceeds analysis compelling to institutional capital providers.

Phase 03

Investor Outreach

Targeted process engaging PE firms, family offices, mezzanine lenders, and banks aligned with your sector, stage, and capital needs.

Phase 04

Term Sheet & Close

Negotiate term sheet economics, governance provisions, and anti-dilution mechanics. Manage investor due diligence through to closing.
